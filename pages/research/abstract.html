With the advent of the personal computer in 1981, the field of graphic design has entered into an exponentially evolving shift in meaning, as we’ve been assigned the task of transcribing both the real and the virtual, mapping the terrain of reality. The goal of this project is to analyze and understand the ways in which human individuals are emotionally dependent on virtual stimuli, in hopes of furthering the designers understanding of, and how to utilize, said stimuli. By way of prototyping through various virtual media types and iterative element (or stimuli-based) alterations, this project aims to target and measure human sensory response in an effort to link them to their respective and presupposed emotional dependencies in order to further understand how they influence actions, thoughts, behaviors, and feelings. Furthermore this project looks to codify the relationship between virtual stimuli and the psyche using qualitative research methods and prototypical creations in order to merit it’s conclusions.
